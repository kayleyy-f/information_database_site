Overview

Orientation early August; classes begin immediately after. Urban academic medical center in Jackson. AMCAS primary deadline Oct 15; secondary by Nov 15. Mission: train Mississippi physicians through education, research, and patient care.

Admissions

AMCAS primary by Oct 15; EDP by Aug 1. Rolling holistic review starting August—100% MS residents; minimal non-residents. Secondary $100 by Nov 15. Interview panel style Sept–Feb.

Acceptance Data

Class size ~155, all MS residents. Avg MCAT ~506; avg GPA ~3.8. State law mandates in-state priority—resident applicants have ~38% acceptance, overall ~5%.

Education & Research

Traditional 4-year MD with systems-based preclinical and community/rural clerkships. Required scholarly activity; MD/PhD, MD/MPH, and MD/MBA options. Strong translational and public health research at an R1 center.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

In-state tuition ~$35,200; non-resident ~$72,000 (rare). Avg graduate debt ~$180,000. Robust state scholarship programs (Rural Physicians Scholarship), MRPSP, MRPSP, and federal loans.

Campus Life

UMMC campus in Jackson’s Fondren district—urban medical center with arts-y neighborhood. Affordable off-campus housing. Tight-knit cohort (all in-state), 40+ orgs, Jackson Free Clinic service, intramurals, Cadaver Ball, strong support services and traditions.
