Overview

Accelerated start mid-July (orientation early July). Four suburban campuses in Blacksburg VA, Spartanburg SC, Auburn AL, Monroe LA, each on/near partner university grounds. AACOMAS primary by April 10; secondary by April 15. Mission: prepare community-focused osteopathic physicians for rural and underserved regions, advancing research and global health.

Admissions

AACOMAS primary by April (rolling from June); secondary $30 by April 15. Minimum ~3.2 GPA/498 MCAT, mission-fit emphasis on rural/underserved. Campus preference ranked on secondary; rolling interviews Aug–April; deposit $1,500.

Acceptance Data

Each campus ~150–185 matriculants (total ~650+). Avg MCAT ~501; avg GPA ~3.66. Heavy SE US representation; strong rural background among students. Overall match ~99%; COMLEX pass rates ~92–96%.

Education & Research

Systems-integrated DO curriculum with early clinical exposure and OMM labs. Required scholarly activity; optional Honors Research Track. Strong rural continuity clinics; global mission trips; simulation centers; dual DO/MPH and DO/MBA options.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

Tuition $51,800/year (flat for all). No extra mandatory fees. COA ~$80k/year including living. ~84% take loans; avg grad debt ~$231,000. Low scholarship amounts; many use NHSC/military forgiveness programs.

Campus Life

Suburban campuses each tied to a large university—access to athletics, rec centers, libraries. Strong service culture: free clinics, health fairs, intramural sports, global outreach. Supportive cohorts with mission-driven traditions and wellness programming.
