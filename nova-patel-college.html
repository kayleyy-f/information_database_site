Overview

Late July/August start. Suburban campuses in Fort Lauderdale/Davie and Clearwater (Tampa Bay). AACOMAS primary by Jan 15; secondary by Mar 1. Mission: learner-centered osteopathic education producing compassionate, competent physicians.

Admissions

AACOMAS rolling—apply early summer. Secondary $50, due ~Dec 15. No strict cutoffs (median MCAT ~504, GPA ~3.70). MD letter recommended. Interviews campus-specific, rolling from Aug through spring.

Acceptance Data

Class of ~380 total (260 Davie, 120 Tampa). ~7,000 AACOMAS apps; ~1,000 interviews; ~400 matriculants (~5.5% acceptance). Avg MCAT ~504.5; avg GPA ~3.70; diverse geographic mix (40–50% Florida residents).

Education & Research

Hybrid systems-integrated preclinical with OMM labs and ultrasound training. Early clinical skills via standardized patients and robust simulation centers. Cl 3–4 community and rural clerkships across Florida. Optional research elective, honors track, DO/MPH and DO/MBS dual degrees, strong interprofessional experiences with NSU MD, dental, and health professions.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

Tuition $69,870; fees ~$3,735; insurance ~$2,270 (2024–25). COA ~$78k/year total. ~84% finance via loans; avg graduate debt ~$349,000. Limited internal scholarships; many pursue NHSC or military HPSP for forgiveness.

Campus Life

Large suburban university campuses with full NSU amenities (RecPlex, library, dining). DO student culture is collaborative and service-oriented—multiple clubs, interprofessional events, intramurals, community outreach (free clinics, health fairs), global mission trips, and strong wellness support.
