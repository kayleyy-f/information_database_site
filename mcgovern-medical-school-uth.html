Overview

  • Classes begin: Early August (first-year class starts first week of August after late-July orientation).
  • Campus type: Urban (in the heart of Houston’s Texas Medical Center, surrounded by major hospitals & research institutes).
  • Application deadline: TMDSAS primary due October 1; secondary typically due November 15.
  • Mission: “To provide the highest quality of education and training of future physicians for the State of Texas,” along with a commitment to cutting-edge research and excellent patient care. McGovern’s mission includes training a diverse physician workforce and serving community needs through innovative healthcare.

Admissions

  • Primary application deadline: October 1 (TMDSAS; no late primaries accepted).
  • Review begins: July/August on a rolling basis (interviews as early as late August or September).
  • Application system: TMDSAS (for both in-state & out-of-state applicants).
  • Secondary application fee: $60 (fee waivers available for financial need).
  • Secondary application deadline: Typically November 15.

Acceptance Data

  • Median MCAT: ~512 (≈85th percentile).
  • Median GPA: ~3.85.

Education & Research

McGovern offers a two-year foundational science curriculum followed by two years of clinical rotations. Pre-clinical active-learning includes case-based sessions and simulation labs. Research opportunities abound via affiliations with MD Anderson, Memorial Hermann, the Brown Foundation Institute of Molecular Medicine, and other TMC institutions. Students may join summer research programs, pursue an optional Scholarly Concentration (e.g. Global Health, Medical Education), or participate in quality improvement projects. While research is not required for graduation, many students engage in projects under faculty mentorship, reflecting McGovern’s mission to advance discovery alongside clinical training.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

  • In-state cost of attendance: Tuition & fees ≈ $26,094; total COA ≈ $62,000/year.
  • Out-of-state cost of attendance: Tuition & fees ≈ $33,749; total COA ≈ $69,000/year (non-residents may qualify for in-state rate after meeting criteria).

Campus Life

Setting: Immersed in a dynamic urban environment, students can walk from lectures to hospital rounds within TMC. Houston’s arts, sports, parks, and food scene offer plenty of off-duty options.

Housing: No on-campus dorms; students rent nearby in TMC area, Midtown, Montrose, or West University. UTHealth Student Residences and other subsidized options exist. METRO rail, biking, and walking are feasible.

Culture: Class size ~240 fosters a collaborative “advisory college” system for mentoring across years. 50+ student organizations (clinical interest, service, wellness, leadership), HOMES Clinic for Houston’s homeless, and inter-school activities with other TMC institutions. Wellness is emphasized via intramural sports, fitness center access, and student-run events.
