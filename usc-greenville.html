Overview

Classes begin in early July (orientation late June). Urban campus on the Prisma Health Greenville Memorial Hospital campus. Applies via AMCAS—primary deadline Nov 1; secondary Dec 15. Mission: “Educate. Innovate. Serve. Where Lifestyle is Medicine,” training physicians to improve community health.

Admissions

AMCAS primary deadline Nov 1; Early Decision by Aug 1. Rolling review June–Oct; interviews Aug–spring. Secondary fee $95, due Dec 15. ~70% in-state preference; US citizens/permanent residents only.

Acceptance Data

Class size ~110. Avg MCAT ~510–511; avg GPA ~3.7–3.75. ~5,000+ applications yield ~110 matriculants (30% non-SC residents).

Education & Research

Innovative integrated curriculum with EMT certification in first semester and a Lifestyle Medicine thread. Research optional via Scholarly Activity program and Med School Scholars track; ~70% participate in research or publish.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

In-state tuition ~$43,800/year; out-of-state ~$88,000/year. Cost of attendance ~$70k in-state, ~$110k out-of-state. ~85% receive aid; avg graduate debt ~$200k. Scholarships and state loan-forgiveness available.

Campus Life

Urban hospital campus with modern education building, simulation center, and on-campus ambulance bay. Off-campus housing in downtown or West End. Strong “family” culture, dozens of orgs (Root Cause free clinic, AMA, GHHS). Greenville’s thriving downtown, trails, and proximity to mountains/beach enrich student life.
