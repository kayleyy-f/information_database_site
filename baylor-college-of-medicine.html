Overview

  • Classes begin: Mid-July (orientation mid-July; classes start late July).
  • Campus type: Urban (Houston’s Texas Medical Center).
  • Application deadline: Primary via TMDSAS due November 1; supplemental due November 15.
  • Mission: “Baylor College of Medicine is a health sciences university that creates knowledge and applies science and discoveries to further education, healthcare and community service locally and globally.”

Admissions

  • Primary application deadline: November 1 (TMDSAS, 5 p.m. CST).
  • Review begins: July (rolling through December).
  • Application system: TMDSAS (Texas Medical & Dental Schools Application Service).
  • Secondary application fee: $100 (supplemental).
  • Secondary application deadline: November 15 (5 p.m. CST).

Acceptance Data

  • Median MCAT: ~518 (97th percentile).
  • Median GPA: ~3.9.

Education & Research

Baylor is a research-intensive institution “dedicated to bringing discoveries from bench to bedside.” Students have extensive opportunities via the Student Opportunities for Advancement in Research (SOAR) program. The five-year Medical Research Pathway adds an extra year of intensive research training. Research is highly valued; students are encouraged to engage in laboratory or clinical projects, and Baylor’s affiliation with top Texas Medical Center hospitals provides rich resources and mentorship.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

  • Tuition & fees: $26,622 in-state | $41,788 out-of-state (2025–26).
  • Combined degree options: MD/PhD, MD/MPH, MD/MBA.

Campus Life

  • Located in the world’s largest medical complex, with on-site simulation centers, libraries, and free clinics.
  • Over 50 student organizations covering clinical interest, service, wellness, and leadership.
  • Strong support services, wellness programming, and community outreach through student-run clinics.
