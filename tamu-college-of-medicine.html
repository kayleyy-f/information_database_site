Overview

  • Classes begin: Early August (orientation in late July).
  • Campus type: Multi-campus system. Pre-clinical in Bryan/College Station (college-town suburban), clinical rotations at Temple, Dallas, Houston, Round Rock.
  • Application deadline: TMDSAS primary due October 1; secondary due mid-November (≈Nov 15).
  • Mission: “Our mission is to improve the health and well-being of the people of Texas through excellence in education, research and health care delivery,” guided by Aggie values of excellence, integrity, and leadership.

Admissions

  • Primary application deadline: October 1 (TMDSAS).
  • Review begins: July/August on a rolling basis; interviews offered early fall.
  • Application system: TMDSAS for all applicants.
  • Secondary application fee: $60 (waivers accepted for TMDSAS fee-waiver holders).
  • Secondary application deadline: November 15.

Acceptance Data

  • Median MCAT: ~512.
  • Median GPA: ~3.86.

Education & Research

Pre-clinical curriculum can be taken in Bryan/College Station or at a distributed site. Clinical years rotate through five campuses—from large urban centers (Dallas, Houston) to smaller cities (Temple, Round Rock). Research opportunities include the Summer Research Program, the Research Honors Program, and community-focused projects (telemedicine for rural clinics, diabetes, obesity). EnMed (MD/MEng) is offered separately; MD/PhD and MD/MPH tracks are also available within the Texas A&M system. The college’s emphasis on rural, military, and underserved-population health aligns research with its mission.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

  • In-state COA: Tuition & fees ≈ $21,760; total COA ≈ $52,000/year.
  • Out-of-state COA: Tuition & fees ≈ $34,860; total COA ≈ $65,000/year (non-residents often qualify for in-state after year 1).

Campus Life

Setting: Bryan/College Station offers classic college-town life (Aggie traditions, SEC football, Muster, Midnight Yell). During clinical years, students live in Temple, Dallas, Houston, or Round Rock—each with its own local amenities.

Housing: Apartments and houses near each campus; Bryan has low cost of living, clinical sites offer guided listings and on-site options. Commuting by car is common; parking is provided at all campuses.

Culture: A unified Aggie spirit despite geographic spread—shared White Coat Ceremony, student government representation at each site, and common events. Student organizations cover specialties, service, wellness, and community outreach. Strong alumni network (“Aggie Network”) and inter-campus mentoring keep students connected.
