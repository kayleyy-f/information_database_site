Overview

Classes begin mid-July (orientation July 7–11, 2025; classes start July 14). The suburban/urban Fort Worth campus sits in the Cultural District. Primary application via TMDSAS due October 1; secondary due October 15. Mission: “Create solutions for a healthier community by preparing tomorrow’s patient-centered physicians and scientists and advancing the continuum of medical knowledge, discovery, and osteopathic health care.”

Admissions

Apply via TMDSAS (opens May 15; deadline October 1). Rolling review begins in summer; Early Decision by August 1 (notifications by Sept 15). Secondary is $50, due October 15. 90% of seats reserved for Texas residents. Prerequisites and letters of evaluation required.

Acceptance Data

Class size ~231. For Fall 2023, avg MCAT 507.8 and avg GPA 3.83. ~650–750 interviews granted each cycle for ~230 seats.

Education & Research

Four-year DO curriculum with strong primary care focus. Offers Rural Osteopathic Medical Education (ROME) program, Summer Research Fellowship, Honors Research Course, and a DO/PhD pathway in partnership with UNT.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

In-state tuition $13,078/year; out-of-state $28,766; fees ~$6,690; insurance ~$2,868 (2025–26). Avg graduate debt ~$110,000. ~87% receive financial aid. Multiple internal scholarships plus state loan-repayment programs available.

Campus Life

Urban-suburban campus with no on-campus dorms—students rent nearby. “Advisory colleges” foster mentoring and wellness. Over 50 student orgs (specialty interest, service, wellness). Facilities include a modern simulation center, 24/7 anatomy lab, interprofessional library. Strong community engagement via free clinics and the Primary Care Pathway.
