Overview

  • Orientation: July 7–11, 2025; classes begin July 14, 2025.
  • Campus type: Suburban/urban campus in Fort Worth’s Cultural District.
  • Application deadline: TMDSAS primary due October 1; secondary due October 15.
  • Mission: “Create solutions for a healthier community by preparing tomorrow’s patient-centered physicians and scientists and advancing the continuum of medical knowledge, discovery, and osteopathic health care.”

Admissions

  • Application system: TMDSAS (opens May 15; due October 1).
  • Review process: Rolling review begins summer; Early Decision by August 1 (notifications by Sept 15).
  • Secondary fee: $50 due October 15.
  • Seat reservation: ~90% of seats reserved for Texas residents.
  • Requirements: Prerequisite courses and letters of evaluation.

Acceptance Data

  • Class size: ~231 students.
  • Median MCAT: 507.8.
  • Median GPA: 3.83.

Education & Research

This four-year DO curriculum emphasizes primary care and rural health. Offers the Rural Osteopathic Medical Education (ROME) program, a Summer Research Fellowship, and an Honors Research Course. A DO/PhD pathway is available in partnership with UNT.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

  • Tuition: In-state $13,078/year; out-of-state $28,766/year.
  • Fees: ~$6,690; insurance ~$2,868 (2025–26).
  • Average debt: ~$110,000.
  • Financial aid: ~87% receive aid or scholarships.

Campus Life

  • No on-campus dorms; students rent nearby.
  • Advisory colleges for mentoring and wellness support.
  • 50+ student organizations (clinical, service, wellness, leadership).
  • Facilities include a modern simulation center, 24/7 anatomy lab, interprofessional library, and student-run free clinics.
