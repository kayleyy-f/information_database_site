Long School of Medicine – UT Health San Antonio

Overview

  • Classes begin: Early August (orientation typically in late July, with classes for first-year medical students starting by the first week of August).
  • Campus type: Urban. The Long School of Medicine is located in San Antonio’s South Texas Medical Center area, a large medical/clinical district within the city.
  • Application deadline: Primary application due Oct. 1 (via TMDSAS, in line with Texas medical school deadlines), with secondary application deadlines usually in mid-fall (around Nov. 15).
  • Mission: “To provide responsive and comprehensive education, research and service of the highest quality in order to meet the health-related needs of the citizens of Texas,” with a particular focus on South Texas:contentReference[oaicite:25]{index=25}. The Long SOM emphasizes diversity, professionalism, and commitment to the South Texas region in its mission:contentReference[oaicite:26]{index=26}.

Admissions

  • Primary application deadline: October 1 (TMDSAS primary application deadline for UT San Antonio’s Long SOM).
  • Application review start date: Late summer. The admissions committee begins reviewing applications in August on a rolling basis; interview invitations are typically sent out starting in early fall.
  • Application system: TMDSAS. All applicants (Texas residents and non-residents) apply through the Texas Medical & Dental Schools Application Service.
  • Secondary application fee: $60. (The Long SOM secondary application fee is around $60; fee waivers are available for those with TMDSAS fee waivers.)
  • Secondary application deadline: November 15. (Applicants are expected to complete the UT San Antonio-specific secondary essays and submit the fee by mid-November.)

Acceptance Data

  • Median MCAT: ~518. The entering class at Long SOM has a high MCAT median/average (around 517–518):contentReference[oaicite:27]{index=27}, reflecting a competitive applicant pool.
  • Median GPA: ~3.88. The median undergraduate GPA is about 3.8–3.9 for recent classes:contentReference[oaicite:28]{index=28}.

Education & Research

UT Health San Antonio’s Long School of Medicine offers a comprehensive education with significant opportunities for clinical experience and research. The first two years emphasize an integrated curriculum with organ system-based courses and early clinical exposure. Students start interacting with patients early via the Longitudinal Clinical Experience program. Research is an important pillar: Long SOM encourages student research through summer programs and an optional research track. The school hosts the Dean’s Summer Research Program where between M1 and M2 year, students can work with faculty on biomedical or clinical research projects and often present at research symposia. Areas of research strength include cancer (through the Mays Cancer Center), diabetes, and aging (the Barshop Institute for Longevity and Aging). Long SOM is also known for its strong focus on community health research – many students engage in projects addressing South Texas health issues like border health, infectious diseases, or rural medicine. Overall, while the school’s primary focus is producing excellent clinicians, it also provides robust research training for those interested. Students can pursue dual degrees (MD/MPH, MD/PhD) if they seek extensive research careers. The importance of research at Long SOM is underscored by its mission to advance scholarship and its numerous research centers; however, the culture remains one where students not pursuing research still receive strong support to become outstanding physicians.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

  • Cost of attendance (In-state): Tuition and fees approximately $26,619 per year for Texas residents:contentReference[oaicite:29]{index=29}. (UT San Antonio’s med school has one of the lowest tuitions in the country; total in-state COA with living expenses is roughly $59k/year.)
  • Cost of attendance (Out-of-state): Tuition and fees approximately $42,035 per year for non-residents:contentReference[oaicite:30]{index=30}. (Out-of-state students may become eligible for Texas residency after one year, which can greatly reduce tuition in subsequent years.)

Campus Life

Setting: The Long School of Medicine is part of the UT Health San Antonio campus in the city’s Medical Center district. This setting offers an “academic health center” environment – multiple hospitals (University Hospital, VA, etc.) and clinics are within walking distance, giving students easy access to clinical learning opportunities. San Antonio itself is a large city with a rich cultural heritage, famous for the River Walk, Tex-Mex cuisine, and a relaxed, friendly atmosphere.

Housing: Many Long SOM students live in apartments or houses in the neighborhoods surrounding the Medical Center (such as Oak Hills or near Leon Valley). The cost of living in San Antonio is comparatively affordable; students often find modern apartments with amenities at reasonable rates. While the university does not provide on-campus dorms for med students, UT Health San Antonio assists with off-campus housing resources. Some students choose to live in other parts of the city (downtown or Alamo Heights) and commute, typically by car, as public transport in San Antonio is limited. Parking is available on campus for students, which makes commuting convenient.

Campus culture: Long SOM prides itself on a supportive and collegial culture. The student body is diverse and united by a strong camaraderie—students often study in groups and form tight-knit bonds through the intensity of medical training. There are dozens of student organizations, including specialty interest groups (Surgery Club, Pediatrics Club, etc.), service organizations (like the Frontera de Salud which provides care to underserved border communities), and committees such as the Wellness and Fitness Committee that organizes intramural sports and wellness events. UT Health San Antonio has a tradition of community service; medical students run free clinics and health screenings in San Antonio, reflecting the school’s mission focus on South Texas. The campus also offers opportunities for leadership (student government, honor societies such as AOA and the Gold Humanism Honor Society). San Antonio’s culture – warm, family-oriented, and celebratory – permeates student life: the school hosts an annual Fiesta Med event in spring to coincide with the city’s Fiesta celebrations, and students frequently gather to enjoy the city’s famous barbecue or attend Spurs basketball games. Overall, life at Long SOM balances rigorous academics with a down-to-earth, friendly environment where peers and faculty alike treat each other like family, and where the surrounding community’s needs and culture deeply enrich the medical education experience.
