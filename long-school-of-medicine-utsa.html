Overview

  • Classes begin: Early August (orientation in late July; classes start first week of August).
  • Campus type: Urban (in San Antonio’s South Texas Medical Center district).
  • Application deadline: TMDSAS primary due October 1; secondary due mid-November (around November 15).
  • Mission: “To provide responsive and comprehensive education, research and service of the highest quality in order to meet the health-related needs of the citizens of Texas,” with a focus on South Texas. The Long SOM emphasizes diversity, professionalism, and commitment to its region.

Admissions

  • Primary application deadline: October 1 (TMDSAS).
  • Review begins: August on a rolling basis; interviews begin early fall.
  • Application system: TMDSAS for all applicants.
  • Secondary application fee: $60 (waivers available with TMDSAS waiver).
  • Secondary application deadline: November 15.

Acceptance Data

  • Median MCAT: ~517–518.
  • Median GPA: ~3.88.

Education & Research

The first two years use an integrated, system-based curriculum with early clinical exposure through the Longitudinal Clinical Experience. Students may join the Dean’s Summer Research Program between M1 and M2, working with faculty on biomedical or clinical projects and presenting at symposia. Research strengths include cancer (Mays Cancer Center), diabetes, aging (Barshop Institute), and community health. Optional MD/MPH and MD/PhD tracks are available for those pursuing in-depth research careers.

Tuition, Aid & Debt

  • In-state COA: Tuition & fees ≈ $26,619; total ≈ $59,000/year (including living expenses).
  • Out-of-state COA: Tuition & fees ≈ $42,035; total ≈ $75,000/year (non-residents may qualify for in-state after one year).

Campus Life

Setting: Academic Health Center environment—hospitals and clinics within walking distance in the Medical Center district. San Antonio offers a rich cultural scene, the River Walk, Tex-Mex cuisine, and a friendly, affordable city atmosphere.

Housing: No on-campus dorms; students rent in nearby neighborhoods (Oak Hills, Leon Valley, Alamo Heights). UT Health assists with off-campus listings; most commute by car, and parking is plentiful.

Culture: Supportive, collegial atmosphere with dozens of student organizations (specialty interest, service, wellness). Active community service through clinics like Frontera de Salud. Campus traditions include Fiesta Med events and wellness programming to balance academics and life.
